City Latitude Vs Cloudiness

This graph shows whether a cities latitude and closness to the equator affects the cloudiness. Since the plots are all over the graph it can be concluded that there is no significant correlation between the percent of cloudiness and a cities latitude.
According to http://www-das.uwyo.edu/~geerts/cwx/notes/chap08/cloud_lat.html#:~:text=Satellite%2Dbased%20cloudiness%20estimates%20over,%2D%2050%25%20cloud%20cover%20there.&text=At%20latitudes%20above%2050%EF%BF%BD,cloud%20cover%20is%20about%2070%25. cloudiness does have to deal with latitude and longitude, but it not as simple as being close to the equator increases or decreases the cloudiness. In fact in one instance the equatorial belt actually throws off the norm of cloudiness used for the rest of the globe.
The image above was found at https://slate.com/technology/2015/05/cloudy-earth-map-of-cloud-coverage.html on 11/17/2020.
